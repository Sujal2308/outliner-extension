What is a DDoS attack?

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal traffic of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming the target or its surrounding infrastructure with a flood of Internet traffic.

DDoS attacks achieve effectiveness by utilizing multiple compromised computer systems as sources of attack traffic. Exploited machines can include computers and other networked resources such as IoT devices.

How DDoS Attacks Work

From a high level, a DDoS attack is like an unexpected traffic jam clogging up the highway, preventing regular traffic from arriving at its destination. The attack traffic consists of seemingly legitimate requests, making it difficult to separate the attack traffic from normal traffic.

DDoS attacks are carried out with networks of Internet-connected machines. These networks consist of computers and other devices (such as IoT devices) which have been infected with malware, allowing them to be controlled remotely by an attacker. These individual devices are called bots (or zombies), and a group of bots is called a botnet.

Types of DDoS Attacks

DDoS attacks can be broadly categorized based on the components of network connection they target. The three main categories are:

Volume-Based Attacks

Volume-based attacks include UDP floods, ICMP floods, and other spoofed-packet floods. The attack's goal is to saturate the bandwidth of the attacked site, and magnitude is measured in bits per second (Bps).

Protocol Attacks

Protocol attacks include SYN floods, fragmented packet attacks, Ping of Death, Smurf DDoS and more. This category of attacks consumes actual server resources, or those of intermediate communication equipment, such as firewalls and load balancers, and is measured in packets per second (Pps).

Application Layer Attacks

Application layer attacks include low-and-slow attacks, GET/POST floods, attacks that target Apache, Windows or OpenBSD vulnerabilities and more. Comprised of seemingly legitimate and innocent requests, the goal of these attacks is to crash the web server and is measured in Requests per second (Rps).

DDoS Mitigation Strategies

Effective DDoS mitigation requires a comprehensive approach that includes proper network architecture, traffic analysis, and response protocols. Modern mitigation techniques involve distributed networks that can absorb and filter malicious traffic before it reaches the target.

Cloud-based DDoS protection services have become increasingly important as they can provide the scale and sophistication needed to defend against large-scale attacks. These services typically use machine learning algorithms to distinguish between legitimate and malicious traffic patterns.

Prevention and Best Practices

Organizations should implement multiple layers of protection including firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and traffic monitoring tools. Regular security assessments and incident response planning are essential components of a robust DDoS defense strategy.

Understanding the evolving landscape of DDoS attacks helps organizations better prepare their defenses and respond effectively when attacks occur. As attackers develop new techniques, defensive measures must also continue to evolve.
